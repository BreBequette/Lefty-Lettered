How I Got Started

I’ve always liked creating.

When I was about 4, I had an embroidered wall hanging in my room that said my name in cursive. According to my mom, who I’m sure isn’t biased at all, I made it my mission to copy the swirls and loops until I’d figured out how to write my name in cursive – and I haven’t quit trying to master every arts and crafts project I’ve come across since.

You could say I’ve gone through a lot of phases when it comes to DIY: wood burning, watercolor, floral arrangement and home improvement are just a few of the things I’ve dabbled in before, and still occasionally do. But from the beginning, nothing has stuck quite like lettering.

I decided to get serious about learning to letter in 2016, when I knew I’d want a calligraphy and signage-heavy wedding, and I knew I wanted to do it myself – but as any fellow lefties know, writing (and especially writing nicely) is something our right-handed counterparts take for granted. But I wasn’t deterred: I took in as many left-handed lettering tutorials as I could. I bought guides. And most importantly, I practiced, practiced, practiced. Now I’m ready to put my craft to good use.

If you want to get a taste for my personal style, check out my portfolio. And if you already have a project in mind, drop me a line and let’s bring it to life!

When I’m not lettering…

Or doing any of the other crafty things I’ve already mentioned, you can most likely find me at home with my husband, Leo and dog, Sasha. We’re homebodies who love to snuggle on the couch in front of some crappy TV. I also love to bake, read and explore my city (shoutout, STL).
